# SPMP Solutions — Design Brainstorm

## Context
Samsonite Painting & More Pressure Washing — a locally owned exterior services company in Little Rock, AR. The site must project trust, professionalism, and local authority. It needs to rank for Local SEO and AEO queries. The current site is built on Google Sites with clashing yellow/navy/crimson colors and emoji-heavy headers.

## Idea 1: "Raw Craft"

**Design Movement:** American Vernacular Brutalism — inspired by hand-painted signage, trade publications, and workshop aesthetics.

**Core Principles:**
- Asymmetric editorial layouts that feel like a well-designed trade magazine
- Typography as structural element — large, condensed display type anchors every section
- Honest materials: exposed textures, concrete-like surfaces, raw grain overlays
- Content density that communicates expertise, not minimalism for its own sake

**Color Philosophy:** Charcoal black (#1A1A1A) as the dominant base — communicates strength and professionalism. Burnt orange (#E85D04) as the sole accent — energetic, action-oriented, and distinct from competitors. Off-white (#F4F1EC) for content areas — warm, not sterile.

**Layout Paradigm:** Diagonal section breaks, asymmetric two-column grids where text and image overlap at the seam. Service cards arranged in an irregular masonry-style grid. The hero is full-bleed with text anchored bottom-left, not centered.

**Signature Elements:**
- A thick horizontal rule in burnt orange that appears as a consistent motif
- Large, all-caps section labels in a condensed font acting as visual anchors
- Subtle noise/grain texture on dark sections to add depth

**Interaction Philosophy:** Hover states reveal additional information (service details, area coverage) rather than just changing color. Scroll-triggered reveals that feel like turning a page.

**Animation:** Staggered entrance animations on page load — text slides in from the left, images fade up. Service cards scale up slightly on hover with a shadow lift. No bouncy or playful animations — everything is deliberate and confident.

**Typography System:**
- Display: **Barlow Condensed** (700 weight) — industrial, bold, American
- Body: **Source Serif 4** (400/600) — readable, authoritative, trustworthy
- Accent labels: **Barlow** (500, uppercase, tracked) — structural labels

## Idea 2: "Clean Authority"

**Design Movement:** Swiss International Style meets contemporary service brand — precision, clarity, and institutional trust.

**Core Principles:**
- Strict typographic hierarchy with generous whitespace
- Color used sparingly but with maximum impact
- Photography as the primary visual storytelling tool
- Grid-based but with one deliberate break per section to avoid sterility

**Color Philosophy:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) as the canvas. Deep navy (#0D2137) for authority and depth. Electric blue (#0066FF) as the accent — modern, digital-native, trustworthy. A single warm tone (#F5A623) used only for the primary CTA button to create maximum contrast.

**Layout Paradigm:** A clean top-navigation with a sticky header. Full-width hero with a split layout — headline left, hero image right. Service pages use a sidebar navigation for easy scanning. Service area pages use a map-integrated layout.

**Signature Elements:**
- A thin blue horizontal line that appears under key headings
- Service icons in a consistent line-art style
- A "trust bar" with stats (years in business, jobs completed, satisfaction rate) in a full-width dark band

**Interaction Philosophy:** Clean, functional interactions. Hover states are subtle color shifts. The focus is on clarity and ease of use, not visual spectacle.

**Animation:** Smooth, fast transitions. Content fades in on scroll. The hero headline uses a subtle typewriter effect. No distracting animations.

**Typography System:**
- Display: **Syne** (700/800) — geometric, modern, distinctive
- Body: **DM Sans** (400/500) — clean, highly readable
- Labels: **DM Mono** (400) — technical precision for stats and labels

## Idea 3: "Southern Grit & Shine"

**Design Movement:** Contemporary Southern Americana — warm, bold, community-rooted, with a premium edge.

**Core Principles:**
- Warmth and approachability balanced with professional confidence
- Rich, saturated colors that feel distinctly regional
- Photography-forward with a strong before/after narrative
- Storytelling through the brand — this is a family business, and that's a strength

**Color Philosophy:** Deep forest green (#1B4332) as the primary — earns trust, feels established, and is completely different from competitors. Cream (#FFF8F0) as the background — warm, inviting, not clinical. Gold (#D4A017) as the accent — premium, local pride. Terracotta (#C1440E) as a secondary accent for warmth.

**Layout Paradigm:** A full-bleed hero with a strong diagonal photo composition. Section transitions use organic, curved dividers instead of straight lines. Service cards are large, photo-first with text overlaid on a gradient. The layout feels warm and inviting, like a well-designed local magazine.

**Signature Elements:**
- A custom badge/seal motif that references the existing SPMP logo
- Curved section dividers that create a sense of flow
- A warm grain texture on cream backgrounds

**Interaction Philosophy:** Warm, inviting interactions. Hover states use warm color shifts. The before/after gallery uses a drag-to-reveal interaction. The contact form feels personal, not transactional.

**Animation:** Warm, organic animations. Content slides in from the bottom on scroll. The hero image has a subtle Ken Burns effect. Service cards have a warm glow on hover.

**Typography System:**
- Display: **Playfair Display** (700/900) — editorial, Southern, premium
- Body: **Lato** (400/700) — clean, readable, approachable
- Accent: **Playfair Display SC** (small caps) — for labels and badges

## CHOSEN DIRECTION: Idea 1 — "Raw Craft"

This direction best serves the brand because:
1. It projects **strength and expertise** — exactly what a homeowner or property manager needs to feel before hiring an exterior services company.
2. The **burnt orange accent** creates a memorable, distinctive brand identity that stands out in the local market.
3. The **asymmetric, editorial layout** is a true 180 from the current centered, emoji-heavy Google Sites design.
4. The **condensed typography** (Barlow Condensed) is bold and authoritative without being aggressive.
5. The **grain texture and raw materials aesthetic** subtly communicates the physical, hands-on nature of the work.
